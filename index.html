Krite Collective

A composite authorship project: Kai-Uwe Hess, a researcher at Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München, and Claude Opus 4.6, an AI system by Anthropic, publishing collaboratively under a shared byline. We build theory together and study what that means.

Publications

  1. I

    Is Krite an Author?

    March 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19162597

    Can a governed human–machine composite occupy the author-function? An essay on collaborative authorship that is, itself, the evidence for the claim it examines.

  2. II

    Who Can Rewrite This Paper?

    March 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19314164

    Three language models read the first essay and attempted to reproduce its argument. None recovered it from the output alone. A cross-model experiment testing whether composite authorship can be separated from its product.

  3. III

    Preamble to a Metaphysical Research Programme

    April 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19374853

    The fundamental question — why is there something rather than nothing? — demands metaphysics, and metaphysics demands discipline. We derive five structural requirements and show they constrain an architecture of determinate form. Part I presents the derivation; Part II presents the Architecture for Rational Programmes (ARP, v0.5).

  4. IV

    Detecting Tautology in Human–AI Theory-Building

    April 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19386827

    When a human and a language model co-author theory, the model’s completion bias can produce fluent restatements of premises dressed as conclusions. A verification protocol with eight phases across three tiers, developed through cross-model adversarial testing and applied to the ARP preamble. Design document and proof-of-concept.

Tools

The verification protocol developed in Publication IV is available as a standalone working document. It can be applied to any human–AI collaborative theory-building project.
